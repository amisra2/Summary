          <blockquote class="postcontent restore">
           I was looking at the Libertarian Party website and guess what; Those guys agree with me.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Do you think we should give foreign aid to countries?
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <a href="http://www.lp.org/issues/foreign-policy.html" target="_blank">
            http://www.lp.org/issues/foreign-policy.html
           </a>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Libertarian foreign policy: Ending welfare for nations -- foreign aid
           <br/>
           <br/>
           BY MICHAEL TANNER
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Foreign aid is little more than welfare for nations -- with the same disastrous effects as domestic welfare programs.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           The U.S. currently spends approximately $14 billion per year on foreign aid -- far less than most people believe, but still a substantial sum. Since the end of World War II, the United States has spent more than $400 billion on aid to other countries. But there is little evidence that any of these programs has significantly improved the lives of the people in countries receiving this aid. Instead, foreign aid has typically slowed economic development and created dependence.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Indeed, the U.S. Agency for International Development itself admits,
           <b>
            "Only a handful of countries that started receiving U.S. assistance in the 1950s and 1960s has ever graduated from dependent status."
           </b>
           In fact, despite massive amounts of international aid, the average annual increase in per capita GNP has declined steadily in developing nations since the 1960s, with many of the Third World's heaviest aid recipients actually suffering negative economic growth.
          </blockquote>
