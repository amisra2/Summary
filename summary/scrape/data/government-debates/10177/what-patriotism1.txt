           </i>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           I ask this question mainly for the americans on this forum, mainly because in the UK patriotism isn't really the flashpoint issue that it seems to be in the states. This could be because people overreact to the idea of patriotism because they associate it with Nazi Germany, and also because Britain has already had its decline and fall (to an extent) so we are not so wrapped up in notions of being a 'great' country.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           I also ask with reference to the oft-repeated accusations of someone being 'un-american' or 'anti-american'. This is
           <i>
            definitely
           </i>
           not something you
           <i>
            ever
           </i>
           see in the UK; never in my life have I heard or read someone using the phrase 'anti-British'. I am hard pressed, in fact, to recall a single instance of any culture outside the US using this turn of phrase, with the exception of supranational religion ('un-christian', 'anti-muslim' etc).
           <br/>
           <br/>
           It seems to me to be a meaningless label, particularly (and ironically) so for the 'land of the free' where evryone is supposed to be able to pursue their own crazy lifestyle and espouse their own crazy views without this sort of vilification.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           An extension of this is people being told that they obviously 'hate their own country' etc, with the tacet implication that they are supposed to absolutely love it, a form of flag-worship in my opinion. This makes no sense to me, because as a UK citizen I neither love nor hate my 'country' as a whole. How can I? There are aspects of British life that I love, such as our cynical attitude and irreverent sense of humour, and there are aspects that I hate, such as our readiness to follow the US into wars, and our woefully one-dimensional politics.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           So what do think patriotism is?
           <br/>
           <br/>
           How do you define a patriot?
           <br/>
           <br/>
           What (if anything) does it mean to be 'anti-american'?
