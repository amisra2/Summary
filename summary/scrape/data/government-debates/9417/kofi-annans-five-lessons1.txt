           <div class="bbcode_container">
            <div class="bbcode_quote">
             <div class="quote_container">
              <div class="bbcode_quote_container">
              </div>
              <div class="bbcode_container">
               <div class="bbcode_quote">
                <div class="quote_container">
                 <div class="bbcode_quote_container">
                 </div>
                 Excerpted from
                 <br/>
                 Kofi Annan's Five Lessons
                 <br/>
                 By Kofi Annan
                 <br/>
                 Le Figaro
                 <br/>
                 <a href="http://www.truthout.org/docs_2006/121406G.shtml" target="_blank">
                  http://www.truthout.org/docs_2006/121406G.shtml
                 </a>
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
              "First Lesson: In today's world, we are all responsible for our reciprocal
              <br/>
              security. No nation can assure its own security by attempting to establish
              <br/>
              its supremacy over all the others in the face of threats such as nuclear
              <br/>
              proliferation, climate change, global pandemics or terrorist groups
              <br/>
              operating from safe havens in failed states. Only by working for everyone's
              <br/>
              security may we hope to assure a durable security for ourselves.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Second Lesson: We are also responsible for everyone's well-being. Without
              <br/>
              solidarity, no society can be truly stable. It's not realistic to think that
              <br/>
              a few may continue to derive great advantages from globalization, while
              <br/>
              billions of others are abandoned or plunged into abject poverty. We must
              <br/>
              give all human beings the opportunity to share in our prosperity.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Third Lesson: Security and prosperity depend on respect for human rights and
              <br/>
              a government of laws.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Fourth lesson is that governments must be held responsible for their
              <br/>
              actions, as much on the international as on the domestic scene. Each state
              <br/>
              has accounts to settle with those who have been affected in a decisive way
              <br/>
              by their actions.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              My fifth and final lesson is that these institutions must be organized in a
              <br/>
              just and democratic manner that gives the poor and the weak some influence
              <br/>
              over the actions of the rich and the strong."
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Interesting. What do the you guys think of this? I, for one, agree 100%, but what does everyone else here think?
          </blockquote>
