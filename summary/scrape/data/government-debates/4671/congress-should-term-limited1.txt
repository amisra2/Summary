          <blockquote class="postcontent restore">
           As Congress gets arroganter and arroganter, and their reelection rate hovers
           <br/>
           around 99%, it behooves us to start thinking seriously about restarting the
           <br/>
           drive for a Congressional Term Limits Amendment.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Which is precisely the objective of my fledgling website, tenurecorrupts.com.
           <br/>
           One of the pages on the site is devoted to the various kinds of amendments
           <br/>
           which might be employed by our activists, and to devise the most effective
           <br/>
           wording of a proposed amendment.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           The particular wording I am putting forth (besides specifying 3 terms for the
           <br/>
           House and 2 terms for the Senate) contains the phrase ‘exempts current
           <br/>
           incumbents as long as they are successfully reelected’. The obvious objective
           <br/>
           here is to make the amendment totally unobjectionable to all the incumbents
           <br/>
           who have to vote on the proposed amendment.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           I conceived this weasel wording back in the days when successful incumbent
           <br/>
           reelections were running in the low 90 percentiles, and I figured that if it took
           <br/>
           a few years after ratification for term limits to effectively take place, it was a
           <br/>
           worthwhile tradeoff.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Imagine my horror when I discovered that the 2004 reelection rate was 99%!
           <br/>
           At that rate, replacing only 1%, or 5 or 6 incumbents every 2 years, it would
           <br/>
           take about 50 elections, or 100 years, to get only one-half of Congress
           <br/>
           term limited!
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Of course, the skeptic will say that 2004 was an anomaly. Future reelection
           <br/>
           rates are not likely to be so highly successful! Yeah, sure. In the face of
           <br/>
           electoral historical trends, I would be willing to bet that 99% is gonna be
           <br/>
           far more frequent than 90%. I think the lazy, indifferent voter who votes
           <br/>
           mostly on name recognition is here to stay for a long time, sustaining the
           <br/>
           status quo and the entrenched incumbencies.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Remember, when elections are even reasonably close, it is the fraction of
           <br/>
           ‘stand-pat’ name recognition voters who keep the incumbents in place.
           <br/>
           And they will always be with us.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           It is gonna take term limits to change this situation. So let’s get crackin’!
           <br/>
           <br/>
           I'm Nelson Lee Walker, and I believe our Congress needs Term Limits.
           <br/>
           <a href="http://www.tenurecorrupts.com" target="_blank">
            www.tenurecorrupts.com
           </a>
          </blockquote>
