           Two completely separate line of descendant genes can ally because the alliance helps them succeed, it is not necessary for them to select for each other; but in any case they are selecting for social sophistication. That is why I say humans in acquiring reason have transcended the traditional perception of natural selection. We select for what we think is best, often regardless of the success of our own genes. The desire to help others who help us is nothing more than the trader principle; if someone can help you succeed there is no genealogy requirement.
