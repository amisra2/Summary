               We can do a 'back of the envelope' calculation to get some ballpark numbers:
               <br/>
               <br/>
               I read somewhere that the plate tectonic movement occurred during the flood year. Let's say the average movement of the tectonic plates was equal to the distance from New York to Los Angeles, 2777 miles. This number is way low, but I'll use conservative numbers.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               So, 2777 miles is equal to 7.608 miles per day, and that converts to .1417 meters / second.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               The mass of the continental and oceanic crusts is roughly 2.83 X 10^23 kilograms.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               We know (from Isaac Newton) that kinetic energy is: E = 1/2 mv^2, and plugging the above numbers into the formula yields 2.8 X 10^21 joules of energy.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Since the continents are (effectively) not moving at present, the above kinetic energy had to be dissipated as heat, and that much heat would pretty much boil the oceans. If we factor in magma, volcanoes, etc. the situation becomes more dire.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               So, the choices are:
               <br/>
               <br/>
               1. Centuries old physics is completely out to lunch.
               <br/>
               2. Rapid tectonics did not happen in the past several thousand years
               <br/>
               3. The wealth of evidence indicating tectonic movement is really fake, created several thousand years ago.
               <br/>
               4. The tectonic plates have been moving very slowly over many millions of years.
