        <h2 class="posttitle icon">
         Geology trumps Creationism
        </h2>
        <div class="content">
         <div id="post_message_285590">
          <blockquote class="postcontent restore">
           Actually, a study of geology is often too great a challenge for a Creationist, because the scientific evidence completely refutes a literal interpretation of the biblical account. Here are the stories of some fundamentalist geologists who were forced by the evidence to change their minds:
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <a href="http://members.aol.com/dwise1/cre_ev/creationists.html" target="_blank">
            http://members.aol.com/dwise1/cre_ev/creationists.html
           </a>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           One of the featured ex-Creationist geologists is Glenn R. Morton. The website opens the section on his story thus:
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <div class="bbcode_container">
            <div class="bbcode_quote">
             <div class="quote_container">
              <div class="bbcode_quote_container">
              </div>
              <div style="margin-left:40px">
               Glenn R. Morton is a practicing petroleum geologist and a former young-earth creationist and writer of young-earth creationist articles (27 articles and notes published in the Creation Research Society Quarterly). Even though creation science ended up driving him to verge of becoming an atheist, he is a Christian whose attempts at a scientifically accurate harmonization are described on his web site, DMD Publishing Co.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Morton's story was the first real indication I had that creation science destroys faith. At the 1986 International Conference on Creationism, after he delivered his paper critical of Flood Geology, John Morris of the Institute for Creation Research challenged him. Morton chopped Morris off at the ankles with two questions, the second of which was "How old is the earth?" To which Morris responded, "If the earth is more than 10,000 years old then Scripture has no meaning." Morton then said that he had hired several geology graduates of Christian Heritage College [which formerly housed the ICR], and that all of them suffered severe crises of faith. They were utterly unprepared to face the geological facts every petroleum geologist deals with on a daily basis.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               What I did not know at the time was that
               <b>
                Morton was himself fast approaching his own crisis of faith, because the hard facts of geology kept showing him that the teachings of creation science were utterly false
               </b>
               , teachings that creation science had taught him had to be true if Christianity were to have any meaning. Out of that crisis of faith was born Morton's attempt at harmonization.
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
           Morton himself has a website devoted to his particular journey away from fundamentalist Creationism, together with that of several others here:
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <a href="http://home.entouch.net/dmd/person.htm" target="_blank">
            http://home.entouch.net/dmd/person.htm
           </a>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Morton's own story in his own words is here:
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <a href="http://home.entouch.net/dmd/gstory.htm" target="_blank">
            Why I left Young-earth Creationism--by Glenn Morton
           </a>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Fact is, if you are a geologist, it's pretty hard to stick to fundamentalist Christian beliefs about the book of Genesis, because none of them make any sense in the light of actual data. However, when people choose to put credulous religious belief ahead of science, no amount of data in the world will ever convince them they are wrong.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Marc strikes me as one of these people... there is no amount of evidence you could possibly put together that would shake his faith. This is the antithesis of science.
          </blockquote>
         </div>
