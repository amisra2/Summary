           Perhaps I need to elaborate on what I'm saying here.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           My point is that no one in monotheism likely set up "undetectability" with any intention.  (as I said, "not by any action by 'monotheism' or religious people to do so"--i.e. make it undetectable.)  It's just there, by default, because monotheistic gods tend to be the all-powerful sort that are invisible.  Sure, those gods could have been made up as 'invisible', but I seriously doubt anyone was thinking, "
           <i>
            oh, I better make this one as invisible so it will be convenient to keep scientists from being able to disprove it
           </i>
           ."  Ripskar facetiously said it was "convenient" that it does; I'm simply pointing out that it's there simply because of the traits these ages-old religions (long before science as we know it started challenging them) gave these monotheistic gods.  Invisibility is a trait of monotheistic gods that is there by default simply because of how they are typically described.  Allah and Yahweh are obvious examples.  Maybe it seems 'convenient' to those who would seek to debunk religion, but what do you expect religious people to do, here?  Pretend these religions
           <i>
            didn't
           </i>
           describe their gods in this way--as things like 'spirit' and 'invisible'?
