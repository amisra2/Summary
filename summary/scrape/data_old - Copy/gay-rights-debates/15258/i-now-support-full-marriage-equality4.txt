               Marriage (and only marriage) unites the three core dimensions of parenthood --biological, social and legal --into one pro-child form: the married couple.            You're correct that the U.N. Convention on the Rights of the Child does guarantee certain rights to children, but nothing in the Convention says or requires that the parents be One man and One woman. In fact it specifically added an Article to protect ALL parents from discrimination. And furthermore as of 2009 The United States had still not Signed or Ratified this Convention so as of now we are not bound to follow it.

           Here's the U.N. Convention on the rights of a child, I read most of it and I found nothing in it that even mentions Gay marriage. It refers to the Parents as just that.. THE PARENTS.. whether it be a Gay couple who adopt or a Monogamous couple who gave birth ALL Parents are included.

            For the purposes of the present Convention, a child means every human being below the age of eighteen years unless under the law applicable to the child, majority is attained earlier.

            1. States Parties shall respect and ensure the rights set forth in the present Convention to each child within their jurisdiction without discrimination of any kind,
