           <div class="bbcode_container">

             <div class="quote_container">

              Are each of the following three scenarios just as moral as the other:
              <br/>
              <br/>
              (1) Government forcing a woman to have an abortion;
              <br/>
              (2) Government forcing her not to have an abortion; and
              <br/>
              (3) Government taking no position whatsoever on the question of abortion, leaving the decision to each individual woman.
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
           1 and 2 are immoral, 3 is amoral - better than 1 or 2. Doctors shouldn't 'take no position', and should advise - but ultimately, it is the woman's decision.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           My turn. Are each of the following three scenarios just as moral as the other:
           <br/>
           <br/>
           (1) Government forcing a woman to have a hysterectomy;
           <br/>
           (2) Government forcing her not to have a hysterectomy; and
           <br/>
           (3) Government taking no position whatsoever on the question of hysterectomies, leaving the decision to each individual woman.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           I see no difference between my questions and yours. If anything, a hysterectomy is a more serious matter, because it affects potential for reproduction permanently, rather than the single-instance of abortion.
