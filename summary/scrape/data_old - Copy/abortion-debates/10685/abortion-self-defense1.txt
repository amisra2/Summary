          <blockquote class="postcontent restore">
           Well, not quite self defense, but a similar institute, I'm not sure what it's called in english, but approximately "dire need" would be a possible translation.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Self defense means that an otherwise illegal action made legar when used in defense against an unlawful attack. "Dire need" is similar, except a bit more limited. In our legal system, the distinction is that with dire need, the prevented harm must be greater than the inflicted harm.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           As example, a starving man could not be punished for stealing food to prevent his own death, if he has not caused the starvation.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Applying this to abortion is, of course a more complex issue. By the rule, no doctor could be prosecuted for aborting in case the pregnancy threatens the woman's life, for example.
          </blockquote>
