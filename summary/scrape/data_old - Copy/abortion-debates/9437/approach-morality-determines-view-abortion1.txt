         Approach to morality determines view on abortion

          <blockquote class="postcontent restore">
           In discussions in this forum, I have found that a key differentiator between pro-life and pro-choice proponents is their approach to morality.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Pro-life proponents tend to ultimately have a morality based on moral proclamations - religious or otherwise - that cannot be questioned and need no supporting arguement. The proclamation "All human life is sacred" is the main one which leads to their pro-life stance.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Pro-choice supporters tend not to think in terms of moral proclamations, so proclamations such as "All human life is sacred" mean little to them if not more fully supported by rational arguement.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           What is your general approach to morality and how has that affected your view of abortion rights?
           <br/>
           <br/>
           ---------------
           <br/>
           Starting with myself, my general approach to morality is one that tries to "treat others as I would have myself be treated" and involves empathizing with others to try to understand where they are coming from. I follow no fixed moral proclamations. I simply think it would be illogical for me to treat others with one set of moral rules but expect myself to be treated with another set of moral rules - moral rules should be universally applied for them to be self-consistent.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Where abortion is concerned, "I' did not start to exist before I did so mentally (which would have been sometime in the third trimester). For me, it would therefore have made no difference if I had been aborted in the first or second trimester or if I had never been conceived - either way, I never would have existed. So, I have no problem in general with abortions in the first and second trimester. However, I would strictly limit third trimester abortions since I can empathize with mentally existing beings and would not want them to be killed without very good reason, as I would not want to be killed without good reason.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           -----
           <br/>
           <br/>
           So, what are your views on this issue or your own moral approaches?
          </blockquote>
