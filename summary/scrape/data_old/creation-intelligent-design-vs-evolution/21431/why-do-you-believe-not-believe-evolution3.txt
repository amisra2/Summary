           <br/>

           where John Ioannidis indicates, "Claimed Research Findings May Often Be Simply Accurate Measures of the Prevailing Bias" and also notes, "Several methodologists have pointed out that the high rate of nonreplication (lack of confirmation) of research discoveries is a consequence of the convenient, yet ill-founded strategy of claiming conclusive research findings solely on the basis of a single study assessed by formal statistical significance, typically for a p-value less than 0.05."

However, as you can read at
