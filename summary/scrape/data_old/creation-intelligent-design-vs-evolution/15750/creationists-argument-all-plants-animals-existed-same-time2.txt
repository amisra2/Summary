           Ask him to explain, in detail, how carbon dating works and why, and what the flaws are and why they cast doubt on carbon dating. I suspect that, like many creationists, he actually thinks that all forms of radiometric dating are called "carbon dating." Carbon dating is not flawed. It is, in fact, quite reliable. There are several situations where difficulties can arise, but they are understood and competent researchers avoid them. One major problem is contamination of the sample - a problem that creationists frequently have.

           It depends on what you understand a dinosaur to be. No fossil of anything that most people would call a dinosaur has
           <b>
            ever
           </b>
           been found in any deposit younger than 65 million years. It just so happens that there is a line in sedimentary deposits that can be found all over the world. The line is 65 million years old (it contains elements that can be reliably dated by several radiometric methods and always returns a date around 65 million years). Fossils of all kinds (including dinosaurs) abound below that line. Above that line, fossils are very rare for millions of years. Not even fossils of the surviving dinosaurs, the birds, are found for several million years.
           <br/>

           The books I mention above will give you a good time line on human evolution. Modern humans are less than 200,000 years old. Lucy is 3.2 million years old.

It's just that creationists insert a miracle about 6000 years ago. One problem with carbon dating used to be that the amount of CO2 in the atmosphere was variable. But that is no longer a problem. Radiocarbon dating was first calibrated to about 10,000 years through dendrochronology (tree rings). Ice cores from Antarctica and Greenland were used to calibrate radiocarbon dating even further into the past. Finally, lake varves have confirmed and fine tuned the calibration. But creationists have problems with contamination. Creationists radiocarbon dated samples from the bottom of the Grand Canyon, which is stupid, since the rocks are some 1.6 to 1.8 billion years old. Not suitable for radiocarbon dating since it can only be used on organic matter that is up to 50,000 years old. So what did the creationists date that returned dates of only a few thousand years? Pollen.
